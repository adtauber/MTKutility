The Things You Need to Know

MTKutility is a maintenance app for Bluetooth capable GPS receivers and loggers that use the MTK (GPS) chip.  The MTK GPS devices that it will work with are GPS receivers (no logging) with or without AGPS and GPS loggers with or without AGPS.  It has been tested on physical phones running Android 4.1, 8.1 and 9.

MTKutility uses the Android Navigation Drawer. To show the menu click on the 3 bars at the top left of the screen.  Or slide your finger to the right from the left edge of your screen.  Tap outside the navigation drawer if you do not want to select a new activity.

The Navigation menu will only show actions that your GPS device supports.  You have to connect to your GPS device before the full menu for your GPS device will show.

MTKutility has a custom file explorer built in.   You navigate up the folder hierarchy by clicking the path name to the right of green arrow.  Navigate into folders by clicking the folder name.  Click the icon to select a file.  Files that cannot be selected do not have sel in the icon.  Click your phone's back key to leave.  The file explorer also supports a long press on the icon.  This will expose a popup with View, Rename, and Delete as action choices, and Cancel if you change your mind.  View will only work on files that have viewing support in your phone's apps.

dealing with problems that arise
Your MTK logger is put into binary mode for the AGPS data update.  If MTKutility fails during the AGPS update, your logger will remain in binary mode and will not function as a track point recorder.  Make sure you run MTKutility again to restore NMEA output mode.  MTKutility may be your only means of correcting this.  My Qstarz PC software doesn't.

If you selected the Disable NMEA when downloading preference, a crash while downloading may result in NMEA output being turned off - no scrolling display on the  Home screen when you connect.  Use the Default NMEA button on the Home screen to restore output.

If you selected the Disable GPS logging when downloading preference, you will need to turn your logger off after downloading to restore trackpoint recording capability.  This can't be restored by the app as it overrides the manual setting on loggers that act as either a trackpoint transmitter of trackpoint recorder.

Both the log download and AGPS update do occasionally stop before completion.  This is the result of command/response timing that result in the logger stopping to send responses.  The Download Log and Update AGPS help documents discuss things to try to resolve this problem.

MTkutility has screen rotation turned off and display set to portrait to try an mitigate errors from app interruption.  The Navigation Drawer is also turned off during longer running activities.

Actions to take if MTKutility crashes: cycle your phone's Bluetooth off and on, turn your GPS off and on, and try connecting again.  MTKutility pushes Android Bluetooth capability and implements data exchanges that would be better handled via a USB connection.  However, I have not been able to get the download to work over an OTG connection so Bluetooth is my only option.
